## Context & Angles

1. **Self-Perform Model** — DPR absorbs the document review burden themselves. Unlike design-bid-build where the architect owns docs, DPR's self-perform work means THEY do the coordination. The cost hits their P&L directly.
2. **WND Ventures** — DPR's VC arm invests in construction tech. They have budget and mandate to evaluate new tools. Entry via WND could be faster than enterprise sales.
3. **Complex Project Types** — Healthcare and advanced tech projects have massive, highly regulated document sets. Manual review is a liability.
4. **Innovation Culture** — DPR has innovation managers, R&D team, and tech adoption infrastructure. They're not a laggard.
5. **Regional Structure** — VDC is organized by region. SW Region VDC Leader (Dwane Lindsey) could be an entry point for a pilot.

## Objection Pre-emption

**"We have an internal innovation process for evaluating tools"**
> "Perfect. What's the typical process? Happy to provide a technical brief, security documentation, and a sandbox environment for your evaluation team. We can also run a side-by-side comparison with your current document review workflow."

**"Our IT/security team is cautious about cloud-based AI"**
> "Understood — and appropriate for a company at DPR's scale. We offer fully on-premise deployment where the platform runs in your environment with no external data transmission. Your security team can audit the architecture, and documents never leave DPR-controlled infrastructure."

**"We're already investing in other AI tools"**
> "Great — that means DPR recognizes the opportunity. Our platform is complementary to progress tracking AI (like Buildots) and design AI (like Autodesk tools). We handle the document layer that sits between design and construction. No overlap with tools you're already evaluating."
